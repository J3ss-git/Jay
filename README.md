## Icon 
The icon simple outline of an octopus, which is moving. I gave it eyes representing the umlauts in my surname, a lot of people forget it, and I think it's something I would like to remember and include. Also made the eyes two different sizes, as I do have one weaker eye.
I used blue and orange as my main colours, as blue is the colour representing my theme of the ocean, and orange is one of my favourite colours. I also left the icon rough so that lines don't seem perfectly straight, so that I can show I am human and my flaws make me who I am.

## Infographic 
The infographic represents my icon and follows the same colour scheme used in the design. I intentionally kept the infographic simple and basic to demonstrate that complex or advanced tools are not necessary to communicate identity. Instead, meaning and personal representation can be conveyed through thoughtful design choices.

## Reflection 
Creating a personal logo was challenging because it needed to represent who I am. I chose an octopus because it symbolises adaptability, intelligence, and creativity. The eyes reference the umlaut in my surname and also reflect my own difference in vision. The flowing tentacles resemble ocean waves and subtly form the letter “J” for my first name, Jay. The “J” also extends in different directions, symbolising my dyslexia and the unique way I process information.

Blue was chosen to reflect the ocean theme that I am drawn to, while orange represents my favourite colour and adds energy to the design. I intentionally removed IT-related elements to avoid the stereotype of being “just the IT person,” and instead focused on themes of problem-solving, design, and creativity. Overall, the logo represents my identity, values, and approach to life.
